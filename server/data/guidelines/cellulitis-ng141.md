# Cellulitis and Erysipelas — NICE NG141

## Assessment
- Exclude non-infectious causes (insect bites, venous insufficiency, contact dermatitis)
- Mark extent of erythema with surgical marker pen to monitor progression
- Redness less visible on darker skin tones — assess for warmth, swelling, tenderness

## Antibiotic Treatment
- First-line: flucloxacillin 500mg-1g QDS for 5-7 days
- Penicillin allergy: clarithromycin or doxycycline
- Near eyes/nose: co-amoxiclav (broader cover)
- Review at 2-3 days if no improvement

## Admission Criteria
- Orbital cellulitis
- Suspected osteomyelitis
- Suspected septic arthritis
- Suspected necrotising fasciitis
- Signs or symptoms of sepsis
- Lymphangitis

## Red Flags
- Severe pain out of proportion to the visible infection (consider necrotising fasciitis)
- Rapid progression despite antibiotics
- Crepitus on palpation
- Systemic toxicity (fever, tachycardia, hypotension)

## Reassessment
- No improvement within 2-3 days — reassess diagnosis and antibiotic choice
- Consider alternative diagnoses (DVT, inflammatory conditions)
- Consider IV antibiotics or admission

## Key Pitfalls
- Missing necrotising fasciitis — pain disproportionate to appearance is a critical red flag
- Not marking boundaries — unable to assess progression
- Missing cellulitis on darker skin tones
- Delaying admission when systemic features present
