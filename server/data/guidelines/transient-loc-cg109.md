# Transient Loss of Consciousness — NICE CG109

## Initial Assessment
- Obtain detailed history from patient AND witnesses
- 12-lead ECG
- Vital signs including lying and standing blood pressure

## Uncomplicated Faint (Vasovagal Syncope)
- Prolonged standing or sitting
- Typical trigger present (pain, emotion, heat, prolonged standing)
- Prodrome: sweating, nausea, feeling warm, vision dimming
- Syncope more likely if prodrome abolished by sitting or lying down
- Pallor observed by witnesses

## Urgent Cardiovascular Referral (Within 24 Hours)
- ECG abnormalities (prolonged QT, Brugada pattern, heart block)
- Heart failure
- Exertional syncope
- Family history of sudden cardiac death aged <40
- Heart murmur

## Epilepsy Referral
- Bitten tongue (especially lateral tongue bite)
- Head turning to one side during TLoC
- Prolonged limb jerking (>15 seconds)
- Confusion lasting >5 minutes after event
- Deja vu prodrome

## Investigations
- Ambulatory ECG for suspected arrhythmia (NOT tilt test as first-line)
- Exercise testing urgent within 7 days for exercise-induced syncope
- Consider implantable loop recorder if recurrent unexplained

## Key Pitfalls
- Misdiagnosing cardiac syncope as vasovagal — missing life-threatening arrhythmia
- Misdiagnosing cardiac syncope as epilepsy
- Not obtaining a witness history
- Not performing lying/standing BP
- Delayed cardiovascular referral for red flag features
