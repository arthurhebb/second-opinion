# Hyperkalaemia — Clinical Consensus

## Severity
- Mild: 5.5-5.9 mmol/L
- Moderate: 6.0-6.4 mmol/L
- Severe: ≥6.5 mmol/L

## ECG Changes (Progressive)
- Tall tented T waves (earliest sign)
- Widened QRS complex
- Loss of P waves
- Sine wave pattern
- Cardiac arrest (VF/asystole)
- Always perform 12-lead ECG

## Emergency Treatment (ECG Changes or K+ ≥6.5)
- IV calcium gluconate 10% 10ml over 2-5 minutes
  - Cardiac membrane stabilisation
  - Does NOT lower potassium level
  - Repeat if ECG changes persist

## Lower Potassium (Shift Into Cells)
- Insulin 10 units + glucose 25g IV (onset 15-30 min, check BM at 30, 60 min)
- Salbutamol 10-20mg nebulised (caution in tachycardia/cardiac disease)

## Remove Potassium From Body
- Calcium resonium (oral/rectal)
- Sodium zirconium cyclosilicate (Lokelma)
- Dialysis (for refractory or severe cases)

## Address Underlying Cause
- Stop/review causative drugs: ACE inhibitors, ARBs, spironolactone, NSAIDs, potassium-sparing diuretics
- Treat AKI, CKD, rhabdomyolysis, acidosis as appropriate

## Monitoring
- Repeat K+ within 1-2 hours of treatment
- Cardiac monitoring if severe
- Serial ECGs

## Key Pitfalls
- Not performing ECG — missing dangerous cardiac effects
- Treating with calcium gluconate alone (stabilises but does not lower K+)
- Not stopping causative medications
- Not monitoring glucose after insulin-dextrose
- Not recognising ECG changes
