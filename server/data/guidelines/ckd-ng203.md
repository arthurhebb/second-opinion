# Chronic Kidney Disease — NICE NG203

## GFR Staging
- G1: eGFR ≥90 mL/min (normal or high)
- G2: eGFR 60-89 (mildly decreased)
- G3a: eGFR 45-59 (mildly to moderately decreased)
- G3b: eGFR 30-44 (moderately to severely decreased)
- G4: eGFR 15-29 (severely decreased)
- G5: eGFR <15 (kidney failure)

## ACR Categories
- A1: ACR <3 mg/mmol (normal to mildly increased)
- A2: ACR 3-30 mg/mmol (moderately increased)
- A3: ACR >30 mg/mmol (severely increased)

## Diagnosis Confirmation
- Confirm eGFR <60 with repeat test within 2 weeks (minimum 90 days for full confirmation)

## Referral Criteria
- 5-year renal replacement risk >5% (use kidney failure risk equation)
- ACR ≥70 mg/mmol (unless known cause and appropriately treated)
- ACR >30 with haematuria
- eGFR decline ≥25% within 12 months or sustained decline ≥15 mL/min/year
- Uncontrolled hypertension on 4 or more antihypertensives

## Blood Pressure Targets
- ACR <70: target <140/90 mmHg
- ACR ≥70: target <130/80 mmHg

## ACEi/ARB Therapy
- Offer for ACR >30 mg/mmol, titrate to maximum tolerated dose
- Do NOT start if potassium >5.0 mmol/L
- STOP if potassium ≥6.0 mmol/L
- Monitor creatinine and potassium within 1-2 weeks of starting/changing dose

## Anaemia
- Investigate if Hb ≤110 g/L
- ESA therapy to maintain Hb 100-120 g/L (NOT above 120)
- Check iron status and replete before/with ESA

## Key Pitfalls
- Failing to monitor eGFR trends over time
- Not acting on rising ACR
- Starting ACEi/ARB with potassium >5.0
- Not referring when decline rate criteria are met
- ESA target too high (>120 increases cardiovascular risk)
