# Crohn's Disease — NICE NG129

## Inducing Remission
- First-line: oral glucocorticosteroid (prednisolone)
- Budesonide for distal ileal or ileocaecal disease (less effective but fewer side effects)
- Do NOT use azathioprine, mercaptopurine, or methotrexate as monotherapy for induction

## Maintenance of Remission
- Azathioprine or mercaptopurine as first-line maintenance
- Do NOT use glucocorticosteroids for maintenance (significant long-term side effects)
- Methotrexate as alternative maintenance if thiopurines not tolerated

## Thiopurine Safety
- Check TPMT activity BEFORE starting thiopurines (azathioprine/mercaptopurine)
- Low TPMT activity increases risk of myelosuppression
- Regular blood monitoring (FBC, LFTs)

## Biologic Therapy
- Infliximab or adalimumab for severe active disease
- Unresponsive to conventional therapy (steroids + immunomodulators)
- Consider combination with immunomodulator for infliximab

## Escalation Criteria
- ≥2 exacerbations in 12 months: add azathioprine/mercaptopurine
- Steroid-dependent disease: add immunomodulator or consider biologic

## Surgery
- Consider early for localised distal ileal disease
- May achieve remission with less medication exposure

## Lifestyle
- Smoking cessation is critical — smoking worsens Crohn's disease significantly

## Key Pitfalls
- Using steroids for long-term maintenance
- Delayed escalation to immunomodulators or biologics
- Not checking TPMT before thiopurines
- Not emphasising smoking cessation
- Mismanaging flare with monotherapy immunomodulator (too slow for induction)
