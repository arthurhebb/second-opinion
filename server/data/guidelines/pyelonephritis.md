# Acute Pyelonephritis — NICE NG111

## Pathophysiology
- Upper UTI / kidney infection
- Usually ascending infection from lower urinary tract

## Symptoms
- Loin pain (unilateral flank/back pain)
- Fever and rigors
- Nausea and vomiting
- Lower UTI symptoms may also be present (dysuria, frequency, urgency)

## Investigations
- Midstream urine (MSU) for culture and sensitivity BEFORE starting antibiotics
- Blood tests if admission: FBC, U&Es, CRP, blood cultures

## Antibiotic Treatment
- First-line oral: cefalexin 500mg BD-TDS for 7-10 days OR co-amoxiclav
- Trimethoprim 200mg BD if culture confirms sensitivity
- IV antibiotics if admitted (guided by local protocols)

## Admission Criteria
- Unable to take oral fluids or medications
- Signs of sepsis
- Pregnant
- Significant comorbidity (e.g. diabetes, immunosuppression, renal impairment)
- Symptoms not improving with oral antibiotics

## Key Pitfalls
- Missing pyelonephritis due to atypical presentation without classic loin pain
- Not sending MSU before starting antibiotics
- Treating as simple lower UTI and missing systemic features
- Not admitting pregnant patients or those with sepsis features
