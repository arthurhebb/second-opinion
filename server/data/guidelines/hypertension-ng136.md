# Hypertension — NICE NG136

## Staging
- Stage 1: clinic BP 140/90-159/99 mmHg
- Stage 2: clinic BP 160/100-179/119 mmHg
- Stage 3 (Severe): clinic BP ≥180/120 mmHg

## Diagnosis Confirmation
- ABPM (ambulatory blood pressure monitoring): confirm if ≥135/85 mmHg
- HBPM (home blood pressure monitoring) as alternative

## Treatment Targets
- Under 80 years: <140/90 mmHg
- Aged 80+: <150/90 mmHg

## Step 1
- ACE inhibitor or ARB if: aged <55, or any age with type 2 diabetes
- Calcium channel blocker (CCB) if: aged ≥55, or Black African/Caribbean heritage

## Step 2
- ACEi/ARB + CCB, or ACEi/ARB + thiazide-like diuretic

## Step 3
- Triple therapy: ACEi/ARB + CCB + thiazide-like diuretic

## Step 4 (Resistant Hypertension)
- Add spironolactone (if K+ ≤4.5 mmol/L)
- Or alpha-blocker or beta-blocker
- Seek specialist advice

## Same-Day Specialist Referral (Hypertensive Emergency)
- BP ≥180/120 mmHg WITH any of:
  - Retinal haemorrhage or papilloedema
  - New-onset confusion
  - Chest pain
  - Signs of heart failure
  - Acute kidney injury

## Suspected Phaeochromocytoma
- Labile or postural hypotension
- Headache
- Palpitations
- Pallor
- Diaphoresis

## Key Pitfalls
- Focusing on BP number alone without assessing for target organ damage
- Not confirming with ABPM/HBPM before starting treatment
- Missing secondary causes (phaeochromocytoma, renal artery stenosis)
- Not escalating treatment through steps when targets not met
