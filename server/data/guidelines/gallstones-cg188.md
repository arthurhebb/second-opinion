# Gallstone Disease — NICE CG188

## Investigation
- LFTs and abdominal ultrasound for suspected gallstone disease
- MRCP if USS negative but bile duct dilated or LFTs abnormal (suspected CBD stones)

## Asymptomatic Gallstones
- Reassure — no treatment needed
- Incidental finding on imaging does not require intervention

## Symptomatic Gallstones
- Laparoscopic cholecystectomy is definitive treatment
- Discuss risks and benefits with patient

## Acute Cholecystitis
- Early laparoscopic cholecystectomy within 1 week of diagnosis
- Do NOT delay surgery — early surgery has better outcomes
- Antibiotics if signs of infection

## Surgery Contraindicated
- Percutaneous cholecystostomy if unfit for surgery
- Consider conservative management

## Common Bile Duct (CBD) Stones
- Clear duct surgically (laparoscopic exploration) or via ERCP
- Pre-operative MRCP to assess for CBD stones if suspected

## Ascending Cholangitis
- Charcot's triad: fever, jaundice, right upper quadrant pain
- Medical emergency — requires urgent biliary decompression (ERCP)
- IV antibiotics

## Key Pitfalls
- Missing ascending cholangitis and treating as simple biliary colic
- Delayed cholecystectomy for acute cholecystitis
- Not investigating CBD stones when LFTs abnormal
- Assuming gallstone pain is always right upper quadrant
