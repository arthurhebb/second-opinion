# Pneumothorax — BTS Guidelines

## Presentation
- Sudden-onset pleuritic chest pain
- Breathlessness
- Primary spontaneous: typically tall, thin, young males, smokers

## Tension Pneumothorax — EMERGENCY
- Tracheal deviation (away from affected side)
- Absent breath sounds on affected side
- Haemodynamic compromise (hypotension, tachycardia)
- EMERGENCY needle decompression: 2nd intercostal space, midclavicular line
- Do NOT wait for imaging — clinical diagnosis

## Diagnosis
- Erect chest X-ray in inspiration
- CT chest if uncertain or complex

## BTS Size Measurement
- Measured from lung edge to chest wall at hilum level
- <2cm = small; ≥2cm = large

## Primary Spontaneous Pneumothorax
- Small (<2cm) + not breathless: observe for 4 hours, discharge with safety-net advice
- Large (≥2cm) or breathless: needle aspiration first
- If aspiration fails (still large or breathless): chest drain

## Secondary Pneumothorax (Underlying Lung Disease)
- ALL need admission
- Small (1-2cm) + not breathless: observe with oxygen
- Large (>2cm) or breathless: chest drain
- If age >50 or significant comorbidity: lower threshold for drain

## Key Pitfalls
- Missing tension pneumothorax — attributing symptoms to asthma or anxiety
- Waiting for imaging in suspected tension pneumothorax
- Not admitting secondary pneumothorax patients
- Inadequate safety-netting for discharged patients
