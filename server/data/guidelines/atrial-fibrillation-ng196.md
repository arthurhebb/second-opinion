# Atrial Fibrillation — NICE NG196

## Detection
- Manual pulse palpation for suspected AF (breathlessness, palpitations, syncope, dizziness)
- 12-lead ECG to confirm when irregular pulse detected
- Ambulatory ECG monitoring if paroxysmal AF suspected

## Stroke Risk Assessment
- CHA2DS2-VASc score for stroke risk
- ORBIT score for bleeding risk
- Anticoagulate if CHA2DS2-VASc ≥2 (or ≥1 in males)

## Anticoagulation
- First-line: DOACs (apixaban, dabigatran, edoxaban, rivarexaban)
- Do NOT offer aspirin monotherapy for stroke prevention in AF
- Review anticoagulation at each contact

## Rate Control
- First-line strategy for most patients
- Beta-blocker (e.g. bisoprolol) or rate-limiting CCB (diltiazem or verapamil)
- Do NOT use verapamil with beta-blocker
- Digoxin as add-on if monotherapy insufficient

## Rhythm Control
- Consider if symptoms persist despite adequate rate control
- Electrical cardioversion, pharmacological cardioversion, or ablation
- Flecainide or amiodarone for pharmacological cardioversion

## Emergency Management
- Emergency cardioversion if haemodynamically unstable (e.g. hypotension, chest pain, heart failure)
- Anticoagulation consideration before and after cardioversion

## Key Pitfalls
- Failing to anticoagulate due to bleeding concern overriding stroke risk
- Aspirin is NOT adequate stroke prevention in AF
- Missing paroxysmal AF — consider ambulatory monitoring
- Not checking for underlying cause (thyroid, valvular disease, infection)
