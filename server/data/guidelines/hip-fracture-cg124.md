# Hip Fracture — NICE CG124

## Imaging
- X-ray pelvis and lateral hip as initial investigation
- Offer MRI if hip fracture suspected despite negative X-rays
- CT if MRI unavailable within 24 hours or MRI contraindicated

## Analgesia
- Paracetamol 6-hourly (regular)
- Add opioids if additional pain relief needed
- Consider nerve blocks (fascia iliaca or femoral nerve block)
- Avoid NSAIDs entirely — risk of renal impairment and bleeding

## Surgical Timing
- Surgery on day of admission or the following day
- Do not delay for medical optimisation unless life-threatening condition

## Surgical Approach by Fracture Type
- Displaced intracapsular fracture: arthroplasty (total hip replacement or hemiarthroplasty)
- Trochanteric fracture: sliding hip screw
- Subtrochanteric fracture: intramedullary nail

## Post-Operative Care
- Mobilise day after surgery
- Daily physiotherapy
- Multidisciplinary orthogeriatric care
- Falls risk assessment
- Bone health assessment (DEXA, vitamin D, calcium)

## Key Pitfalls
- Missing occult hip fracture when X-ray is normal — MRI is required
- Using NSAIDs (contraindicated)
- Delayed surgery worsening outcomes
- Not providing adequate analgesia (especially nerve blocks)
- Not assessing for underlying osteoporosis
