# Acute Heart Failure — NICE CG187

## Diagnosis
- BNP <100 ng/L or NT-proBNP <300 ng/L rules out heart failure
- Echocardiography within 48 hours of admission
- Chest X-ray, ECG, blood tests (FBC, U&Es, LFTs, troponin)

## Diuretic Therapy
- IV diuretics (loop diuretics) — bolus or infusion
- Higher doses needed if patient already on oral diuretics
- Monitor fluid balance, weight, renal function

## Medications to AVOID Routinely
- Do NOT routinely use opiates (risk of respiratory depression, nausea)
- Do NOT routinely use IV nitrates (unless specific indication)
- Do NOT routinely use inotropes

## Cardiogenic Shock
- Inotropes and/or vasopressors ONLY for cardiogenic shock
- In level 2 or above care setting (HDU/ICU)

## Ventilation
- Non-invasive ventilation (CPAP/BiPAP) only for severe dyspnoea with acidaemia
- Not as routine treatment

## Beta-Blockers
- Continue or restart beta-blockers once stable
- Ensure at least 48 hours stable before discharge
- Do NOT stop abruptly

## Specialist Input
- Specialist heart failure team involvement for ALL admissions
- Medication review and optimisation before discharge

## Follow-Up
- Follow-up within 2 weeks of discharge
- Ensure medication titration plan in place

## Key Pitfalls
- Misdiagnosing as pneumonia or COPD exacerbation
- Routine use of opiates (outdated practice, harmful)
- Not involving heart failure specialist team
- Stopping beta-blockers during admission
- Inadequate post-discharge follow-up
