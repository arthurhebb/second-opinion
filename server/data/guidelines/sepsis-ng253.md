# NICE NG253 — Sepsis in Adults (16+)

## When to Suspect Sepsis
- Think "could this be sepsis?" when patients present with symptoms suggesting possible infection
- Sepsis may present atypically — patients may feel severely unwell without fever
- Pay attention to observations from patients and caregivers about behavioural changes

## High-Risk Populations
- 75 years or older
- Frail or with multiple chronic conditions
- Immunocompromised (illness or treatment-related)
- Post-surgical within 6 weeks
- Using catheters or repeated antibiotics
- Skin breaches

## Risk Stratification — Community Settings

### High-Risk Indicators
- Objective evidence of new altered mental state
- Respiratory rate ≥25 breaths/min
- Systolic BP ≤90 mmHg or >40 mmHg below baseline
- Heart rate >130 beats/min
- No urine passed in 18+ hours (catheterised: <0.5 ml/kg/hour)
- Mottled/ashen skin appearance
- Cyanosis or non-blanching petechial/purpuric rash

### Moderate-to-High-Risk Indicators
- History of altered behaviour/mental state
- Acute loss of functional ability
- Immunocompromise
- Recent trauma/surgery/invasive procedures
- Respiratory rate 21-24 breaths/min
- Systolic BP 91-100 mmHg
- Heart rate 91-130 beats/min or new arrhythmia
- No urine in 12-18 hours
- Temperature <36°C
- Signs of infection at wound sites

## Risk Stratification — Acute Settings (NEWS2)
- Score ≥7 = HIGH risk
- Score 5-6 = MODERATE risk
- Score 1-4 = LOW risk
- Score 0 = VERY LOW risk
- Any single parameter contributing 3 points requires high-priority clinician review

### Red Flags Overriding NEWS2
- Mottled/ashen appearance
- Non-blanching petechial/purpuric rash
- Cyanosis
- Clinical deterioration since previous assessment

### NEWS2 Recalculation Frequency
- High risk: every 30 minutes
- Moderate risk: every hour
- Low risk: every 4-6 hours

## Managing Suspected Sepsis

### HIGH RISK (NEWS2 ≥7)
- Give broad-spectrum IV antibiotics within 1 hour
- Urgent assessment by FY2+ clinician
- Venous blood tests: blood gas (glucose, lactate), culture, FBC, CRP, U&Es, creatinine, LFTs, clotting screen
- Refer to senior clinical decision maker urgently
- IV fluid bolus without delay (within 1 hour): isotonic crystalloid (Hartmann's or 0.9% saline)
- Initial 250ml bolus over 10-15 minutes, further 250ml boluses as needed up to 1000ml
- If no response within 1 hour: senior decision maker must attend in person
- Refer/discuss with critical care specialist

### MODERATE RISK (NEWS2 5-6)
- Same blood test panel as high risk
- FY2+ clinician review within 1 hour including lactate results
- May defer antibiotics up to 3 hours for diagnostic clarification
- Escalate if deterioration or no improvement

### LOW RISK (NEWS2 1-4)
- Registered practitioner review within 1 hour
- May defer antibiotics up to 6 hours for diagnostic clarity
- Escalate if deterioration
