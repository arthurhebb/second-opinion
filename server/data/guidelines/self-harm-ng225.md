# Self-Harm Assessment — NICE NG225

## Key Principles
- Do NOT use risk assessment tools/scales to predict future self-harm or suicide
- Do NOT use low/medium/high risk stratification for treatment decisions
- Every presentation of self-harm requires individualised psychosocial assessment

## Assessment
- Psychosocial assessment at earliest opportunity by trained mental health professional
- Do NOT delay assessment until after medical treatment is complete
- Do NOT delay assessment until patient is sober
- Assess current mental state, social circumstances, triggers, and intent

## Physical Management
- Follow BNF/TOXBASE guidance for overdose management
- Treat physical injuries appropriately
- Medical management takes priority but does not delay mental health assessment

## Safety Planning
- Collaborative safety plan with the patient
- Identify personal triggers and warning signs
- List coping strategies
- Identify emergency contacts and crisis services
- Reduce access to means

## Therapeutic Interventions
- Offer 4-10 sessions of CBT-informed therapy
- Tailored to the individual

## Admission Criteria
- Safety concerns that cannot be managed in community
- Safeguarding needs identified
- Patient too distressed to engage with assessment or safety planning

## Aftercare
- Follow-up within 48 hours if ongoing safety concerns
- Clear communication between services

## Key Pitfalls
- Using risk stratification tools to determine who gets assessment (everyone should)
- Delaying assessment until "sober" — increases risk of patient leaving
- Dismissing repeat presentations as "attention-seeking"
- Not addressing underlying psychosocial factors
