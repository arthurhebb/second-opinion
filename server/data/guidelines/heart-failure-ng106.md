# NICE NG106 — Chronic Heart Failure in Adults

## When to Suspect Heart Failure
- Breathlessness (exertional, orthopnoea, paroxysmal nocturnal dyspnoea)
- Fatigue, reduced exercise tolerance
- Ankle swelling, weight gain
- History of MI, hypertension, valvular disease, diabetes

## Diagnosis — NT-proBNP Pathway

### NT-proBNP Levels
- **>2000 pg/ml**: urgent referral for specialist assessment and echocardiography within 2 weeks
- **400-2000 pg/ml**: referral for specialist assessment and echocardiography within 6 weeks
- **<400 pg/ml**: heart failure less likely — consider alternative diagnosis

### Key Investigations
- Echocardiography: assess LV function, classify as HFrEF (EF ≤40%), HFmrEF (41-49%), or HFpEF (≥50%)
- 12-lead ECG
- Chest X-ray
- Blood tests: FBC, U&Es, eGFR, LFTs, TFTs, HbA1c, lipids, ferritin/TSAT

## Pharmacological Management — HFrEF (EF ≤40%)

### First-Line Combination (initiate and uptitrate)
- ACE inhibitor (or ARB if ACEi not tolerated) — or ARNI (sacubitril/valsartan) for suitable patients
- Beta-blocker (bisoprolol, carvedilol, or nebivolol)
- Mineralocorticoid receptor antagonist (MRA): spironolactone or eplerenone
- SGLT2 inhibitor (dapagliflozin or empagliflozin)

### Second-Line / Specialist
- ARNI as replacement for ACEi if still symptomatic on optimised therapy
- Ivabradine if sinus rhythm >75 bpm despite maximally tolerated beta-blocker
- Hydralazine + nitrate if ACEi/ARB/ARNI not tolerated (especially Afro-Caribbean patients)
- Device therapy: ICD, CRT where indicated

## Monitoring
- Monitor renal function (creatinine, eGFR) and electrolytes (potassium) after each dose change
- Particular caution with MRA + ACEi/ARB: risk of hyperkalaemia
- Weigh regularly — sudden weight gain may indicate fluid retention
- Monitor for symptomatic hypotension

## Acute Decompensation
- IV diuretics (furosemide) for fluid overload
- Consider non-invasive ventilation if severe pulmonary oedema
- Specialist input for cardiogenic shock

## Common Pitfalls
- Attributing breathlessness to COPD or deconditioning without checking NT-proBNP
- Failing to uptitrate medications to target doses
- Stopping beta-blockers or ACEi due to minor side effects
- Not monitoring renal function after medication changes
- Missing iron deficiency (treat with IV iron if ferritin <100 or ferritin 100-300 with TSAT <20%)
