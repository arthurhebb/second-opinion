# NICE NG148 — Acute Kidney Injury

## Definition and Staging
- **Stage 1**: Creatinine rise ≥26 micromol/L within 48 hours OR 50-99% rise within 7 days OR urine output <0.5 ml/kg/hr for 6-12 hours
- **Stage 2**: Creatinine 100-199% rise within 7 days OR urine output <0.5 ml/kg/hr for ≥12 hours
- **Stage 3**: Creatinine ≥200% rise within 7 days OR rise to ≥354 with acute rise ≥26 OR urine output <0.3 ml/kg/hr for ≥24 hours or anuria for ≥12 hours

## Risk Factors
- Chronic kidney disease (CKD) — strongest risk factor
- Heart failure
- Diabetes
- Liver disease
- Age ≥65
- Nephrotoxic medications: NSAIDs, aminoglycosides, ACEi/ARBs, contrast agents
- Hypovolaemia, sepsis, urinary obstruction

## Detection and Prevention
- Monitor creatinine in at-risk patients during acute illness
- Ensure adequate hydration, especially peri-operatively
- Review and withhold nephrotoxic drugs during acute illness
- **Stop ACEi/ARBs** during acute illness with risk of AKI (sick day rules)

## Assessment and Investigation
- Identify and treat underlying cause: pre-renal (hypovolaemia, sepsis), renal (intrinsic), post-renal (obstruction)
- Urinalysis: haematuria + proteinuria may suggest intrinsic renal disease
- Bloods: U&Es, creatinine, potassium, bicarbonate, calcium, phosphate, FBC, CRP
- **Renal ultrasound within 24 hours** if cause of AKI unknown or urinary obstruction suspected
- Urgent ultrasound if pyonephrosis or obstruction with infection suspected

## Management
- IV fluid resuscitation for hypovolaemia (isotonic crystalloid)
- Treat underlying cause (antibiotics for sepsis, relieve obstruction)
- Stop nephrotoxic medications
- Monitor fluid balance, urine output, and daily U&Es
- Manage hyperkalaemia urgently if present (calcium gluconate, insulin/dextrose, salbutamol)
- Avoid further renal insults (contrast, NSAIDs)

## Referral and Escalation
- **Nephrology referral** for:
  - Stage 3 AKI
  - AKI with no clear cause
  - Suspected intrinsic renal disease (vasculitis, glomerulonephritis)
  - Inadequate response to treatment
  - Renal replacement therapy may be needed
- Urgent dialysis indications: refractory hyperkalaemia, severe acidosis, pulmonary oedema unresponsive to diuretics, uraemic complications

## Common Pitfalls
- Missing AKI because baseline creatinine is unknown
- Continuing ACEi/ARBs and NSAIDs during acute illness
- Attributing low urine output to dehydration without measuring creatinine
- Delayed ultrasound when obstruction is the cause
- Failure to recognise AKI-on-CKD
