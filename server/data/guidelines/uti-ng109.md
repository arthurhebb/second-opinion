# Urinary Tract Infection — NICE NG109

## Pathophysiology
- Lower UTI: bladder infection from GI bacteria ascending via urethra
- Upper UTI (pyelonephritis): infection ascending to kidneys

## Management by Patient Group
- Non-pregnant women: consider back-up (delayed) prescription or immediate antibiotics based on severity
- Pregnant women: immediate antibiotics + send midstream urine (MSU) BEFORE starting
- Men: immediate antibiotics + send MSU before starting

## First-Line Antibiotics
- Nitrofurantoin 100mg MR BD for 3 days (if eGFR ≥45 mL/min)
- OR Trimethoprim 200mg BD for 3 days (if low risk of resistance)

## Red Flags — Consider Hospital Referral
- Symptoms suggesting sepsis
- Fever, rigors
- Rapidly worsening symptoms
- No improvement within 48 hours of antibiotics
- Symptoms suggesting pyelonephritis (loin pain, fever, systemic illness)

## Pyelonephritis
- Refer to hospital if suspected
- See specific pyelonephritis guideline

## Recurrent UTI
- Antibiotic prophylaxis if ≥3 UTIs/year
- Non-antibiotic measures (hydration, post-coital voiding)

## Key Pitfalls
- Missing pyelonephritis or urosepsis by treating as simple lower UTI
- Not sending MSU in men or pregnant women before starting antibiotics
- Nitrofurantoin in reduced renal function (eGFR <45)
- Not safety-netting for worsening symptoms
