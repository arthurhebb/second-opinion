# Status Epilepticus — Clinical Consensus

## Definition
- Continuous seizure lasting >5 minutes
- OR ≥2 seizures without full recovery of consciousness between them

## Initial Approach
- ABCDE assessment
- High-flow oxygen
- Check blood glucose immediately
- IV access

## Stage 1: Early Status (0-5 minutes)
- IV lorazepam 4mg (repeat once after 10 minutes if seizure continues)
- If no IV access: buccal midazolam 10mg OR rectal diazepam 10-20mg

## Stage 2: Established Status (10-25 minutes)
- If seizure continues despite benzodiazepines:
- IV phenytoin 20mg/kg (max rate 50mg/min) with cardiac monitoring
- OR IV levetiracetam 60mg/kg (max 4.5g)
- OR IV sodium valproate 40mg/kg (max 3g)

## Stage 3: Refractory Status (>25 minutes)
- Rapid sequence induction (RSI) and general anaesthesia
- Propofol or thiopentone infusion
- ICU admission
- Continuous EEG monitoring

## Common Causes
- Subtherapeutic antiepileptic drug levels (most common in known epilepsy)
- Alcohol withdrawal
- Metabolic: hyponatraemia, hypoglycaemia, hypocalcaemia
- Structural: stroke, tumour, trauma
- Infection: meningitis, encephalitis

## Investigations
- Blood glucose (immediate)
- FBC, U&Es, calcium, magnesium
- AED drug levels
- Toxicology screen
- CT head
- Lumbar puncture (if infection suspected, after CT)

## Key Pitfalls
- Delayed escalation through treatment stages
- Not checking glucose
- Underdosing phenytoin (weight-based dosing needed)
- Not investigating underlying cause
- Not monitoring cardiac rhythm with phenytoin
