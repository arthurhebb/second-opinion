# Paracetamol Overdose — MHRA/BNF/TOXBASE

## Toxicity Threshold
- Potentially fatal liver damage at ≥75mg/kg in 24 hours
- Staggered overdoses (taken over >1 hour) are higher risk

## Treatment Nomogram
- Single treatment line from 100mg/L at 4 hours to 15mg/L at 15 hours post-ingestion
- Measure serum paracetamol level at 4 hours post-ingestion
- If presenting >4 hours, take level immediately
- Plot on nomogram to determine if treatment needed

## Indications for NAC (N-acetylcysteine)
- Paracetamol level above treatment line on nomogram
- Staggered overdose (any amount over >1 hour period)
- Uncertain timing of ingestion
- Presenting >8 hours after significant ingestion (start NAC, do not wait for level)

## NAC Protocol
- 3-bag IV infusion:
  - Bag 1: 150mg/kg in 200ml over 1 hour
  - Bag 2: 50mg/kg in 500ml over 4 hours
  - Bag 3: 100mg/kg in 1000ml over 16 hours
- Check current BNF for exact protocol (doses and infusion rates updated periodically)
- Anaphylactoid reactions common — slow infusion, treat with antihistamine

## Monitoring
- ALT (liver function)
- INR (coagulation)
- Creatinine (renal function)
- pH (venous blood gas)
- Blood glucose

## Liver Unit Referral Criteria
- INR >3 at 48 hours
- Creatinine rising
- pH <7.3 (metabolic acidosis)
- Encephalopathy
- Hypoglycaemia
- Systolic BP <80 mmHg

## King's College Criteria
- For liver transplant assessment in acute liver failure

## Key Pitfalls
- Missing staggered overdose — patients may not disclose full history
- Relying on a single level for staggered overdoses (nomogram not valid — treat with NAC)
- Delayed NAC in late presenters (>8 hours)
- Not monitoring for delayed liver injury (ALT can rise at 24-72 hours)
- Dismissing "therapeutic" excess as low risk
