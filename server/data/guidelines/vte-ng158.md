# NICE NG158 — Venous Thromboembolic Diseases (DVT & PE)

## DVT Assessment — Wells Score (2-Level)
- Active cancer (ongoing/within 6 months/palliative): +1
- Paralysis/paresis/recent plaster immobilisation: +1
- Bedridden 3+ days OR major surgery within 12 weeks: +1
- Localised tenderness along deep veins: +1
- Entire leg swollen: +1
- Calf swelling ≥3cm vs asymptomatic side: +1
- Pitting oedema (symptomatic leg only): +1
- Collateral superficial veins (non-varicose): +1
- Previously documented DVT: +1
- Alternative diagnosis equally/more likely: -2

**DVT likely: ≥2 points | DVT unlikely: ≤1 point**

### DVT Likely (≥2 Points)
- Proximal leg vein ultrasound within 4 hours + D-dimer if negative
- If ultrasound unavailable within 4 hours: D-dimer → interim anticoagulation → ultrasound within 24 hours

### DVT Unlikely (≤1 Point)
- D-dimer within 4 hours
- If D-dimer delayed: interim anticoagulation while awaiting result

## PE Assessment — Wells Score (2-Level)
- DVT signs/symptoms (leg swelling + deep vein pain): +3
- Alternative diagnosis less likely than PE: +3
- Heart rate >100 bpm: +1.5
- Immobilisation >3 days OR surgery last 4 weeks: +1.5
- Previous DVT/PE: +1.5
- Haemoptysis: +1
- Active/recent malignancy (last 6 months): +1

**PE likely: >4 points | PE unlikely: ≤4 points**

### PE Likely (>4 Points)
- CTPA immediately
- For contrast allergy/severe renal impairment: V/Q SPECT or V/Q planar scan
- If imaging delayed: interim anticoagulation

### PE Unlikely (≤4 Points)
- D-dimer within 4 hours
- If delayed: interim anticoagulation while awaiting
- Positive D-dimer → imaging per likely PE pathway
- Negative D-dimer → stop interim anticoagulation, consider alternatives

## D-Dimer Testing
- Consider age-adjusted threshold for patients >50 years
- Use fully quantitative point-of-care tests only

## Anticoagulation Treatment (Confirmed DVT/PE)
- Minimum 3 months treatment
- First-line: apixaban OR rivaroxaban
- If unsuitable: LMWH ≥5 days → dabigatran or edoxaban
- PE with haemodynamic instability: continuous UFH + consider thrombolytic therapy

## Long-Term Anticoagulation
- Provoked DVT/PE: consider stopping at 3 months if provoking factor resolved
- Unprovoked DVT/PE: consider continuing beyond 3 months; balance recurrence vs bleeding risk
- Annual review: general health, recurrence risk, bleeding risk

## Key Red Flags for PE
- Haemodynamic instability
- Right ventricular dysfunction
- Elevated troponin
- Persistent tachycardia
- Hypoxia disproportionate to clinical picture
