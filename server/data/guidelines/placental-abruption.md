# Placental Abruption — Clinical Consensus

## Definition
- Separation of the placenta from the uterine wall before delivery
- Can be partial or complete

## Presentation
- Sudden severe abdominal pain (constant, unlike contractions)
- Vaginal bleeding (may be concealed — see below)
- "Woody hard" uterus (tense, tender, rigid)
- Maternal shock disproportionate to visible blood loss

## Concealed Abruption — Critical to Recognise
- Blood trapped behind the placenta
- Patient can be shocked with minimal or NO visible vaginal bleeding
- May present with pain, tachycardia, hypotension but little external bleeding
- Most dangerous form — severity easily underestimated

## Risk Factors
- Previous abruption
- Pre-eclampsia
- Smoking
- Cocaine use
- Abdominal trauma
- Thrombophilia

## Investigations
- Clinical diagnosis in emergency — do not delay treatment for imaging
- USS may show retroplacental clot but CANNOT reliably exclude abruption
- CTG for continuous fetal monitoring
- Bloods: FBC, coagulation screen (PT, APTT, fibrinogen), group and save/cross-match

## Management
- ABCDE approach
- Large bore IV access x2
- Cross-match 4+ units packed red cells
- IV fluid resuscitation
- Activate massive transfusion protocol if needed

## Definitive Treatment
- Emergency delivery (usually caesarean section) if:
  - Significant abruption
  - Fetal compromise on CTG
  - Maternal haemodynamic instability

## Complications
- DIC (disseminated intravascular coagulation) — major complication
  - Monitor FBC, coagulation, fibrinogen closely
  - Replace clotting factors, cryoprecipitate, platelets as needed
- Fetal death
- Renal failure

## Rh-Negative Mothers
- Kleihauer test to quantify feto-maternal haemorrhage
- Anti-D immunoglobulin as required

## Key Pitfalls
- Missing concealed abruption — minimal visible bleeding with shocked patient
- Underestimating blood loss when most bleeding is concealed
- Not checking coagulation early (DIC develops rapidly)
- Delaying delivery while waiting for investigations
- Normal USS does NOT exclude abruption
