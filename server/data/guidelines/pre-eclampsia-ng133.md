# Pre-eclampsia — NICE NG133

## Symptoms
- Severe headache
- Vision problems (blurred vision, flashing lights)
- Severe upper abdominal pain (epigastric or right upper quadrant)
- Vomiting
- Rapid swelling (face, hands, feet)

## Blood Pressure Thresholds
- Hypertension: systolic ≥140 mmHg or diastolic ≥90 mmHg
- Severe hypertension: systolic >160 mmHg or diastolic >110 mmHg
- Treatment target: 135/85 mmHg or lower

## Proteinuria
- Protein:creatinine ratio (PCR) ≥30 mg/mmol
- Albumin:creatinine ratio (ACR) ≥8 mg/mmol
- Automated reagent-strip reading for screening

## Magnesium Sulfate for Eclampsia
- Loading dose: 4g IV over 5-15 minutes
- Maintenance: 1g/hr IV infusion for 24 hours
- Recurrent seizures: additional 2-4g IV bolus
- Monitor reflexes, respiratory rate, urine output

## Delivery Timing
- Before 34 weeks: continue surveillance unless severe features
- 34-36+6 weeks: initiate delivery if severe or worsening
- 37+ weeks: initiate birth within 24-48 hours

## Antihypertensives
- First-line: labetalol
- Second-line: nifedipine (if labetalol contraindicated)
- Third-line: methyldopa

## Key Pitfalls
- Attributing symptoms to normal pregnancy complaints (headache, swelling, nausea)
- Not recognising severe hypertension requiring urgent treatment
- Delayed magnesium sulfate in eclampsia
- Missing HELLP syndrome (haemolysis, elevated liver enzymes, low platelets)
