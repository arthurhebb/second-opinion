# Wernicke's Encephalopathy

## Overview
- Acute neuropsychiatric emergency caused by thiamine (vitamin B1) deficiency
- Reversible if treated promptly; irreversible Korsakoff syndrome if delayed or missed
- Significantly underdiagnosed — maintain a high index of suspicion

## Classic Triad
- **Confusion** (altered mental state, disorientation, apathy)
- **Ataxia** (broad-based gait, truncal instability)
- **Ophthalmoplegia** (lateral rectus palsy, nystagmus, conjugate gaze palsies)

**CRITICAL: The complete triad is present in only ~10% of cases.** Confusion alone, or any combination of features, should prompt treatment.

## At-Risk Populations
- Alcohol misuse (most common cause in developed countries)
- Malnutrition or prolonged fasting
- Hyperemesis gravidarum
- Bariatric surgery
- Prolonged vomiting from any cause
- Eating disorders (anorexia nervosa)
- Cancer and chemotherapy
- Refeeding after prolonged starvation
- Patients on prolonged IV glucose without vitamin supplementation

## Critical Management Principle
- **Give IV thiamine BEFORE glucose administration**
- Glucose metabolism consumes thiamine — giving glucose without thiamine can precipitate or worsen Wernicke's encephalopathy
- If glucose must be given urgently (e.g., severe hypoglycaemia), give thiamine simultaneously

## Treatment
- **IV Pabrinex** (high-potency B vitamins including thiamine) — give urgently
- Typical regimen: 2 pairs IV Pabrinex TDS for 3-5 days, then oral thiamine
- IV route preferred — oral absorption of thiamine is unreliable in at-risk patients
- Magnesium replacement (magnesium is a cofactor for thiamine utilisation — deficiency impairs response to thiamine)
- Continue oral thiamine supplementation long-term in at-risk patients

## Diagnosis
- **Diagnosis is clinical** — do NOT delay treatment for investigations
- MRI may show characteristic signal changes in mammillary bodies, medial thalami, periaqueductal grey, and tectal plate
- MRI sensitivity is only ~50% — a normal MRI does not exclude the diagnosis
- Low red cell transketolase activity supports diagnosis but is rarely available acutely

## If Untreated
- Progression to **Korsakoff syndrome**: irreversible anterograde and retrograde amnesia, confabulation
- Korsakoff syndrome develops in ~80% of untreated Wernicke's cases
- Permanent cognitive impairment requiring long-term institutional care

## Common Pitfalls
- Waiting for the complete triad before treating (only present in ~10%)
- Giving IV glucose without thiamine in an at-risk patient
- Using oral thiamine instead of IV in acutely unwell patients (poor absorption)
- Attributing confusion in alcohol-dependent patients solely to intoxication or withdrawal
- Not considering Wernicke's in non-alcoholic patients (hyperemesis, malnutrition, post-bariatric)
- Not replacing magnesium alongside thiamine
- Missing the diagnosis because MRI is normal
