# NICE CG95 — Chest Pain of Recent Onset

## ACS Red Flag Symptoms
- Chest/arm/back/jaw pain lasting >15 minutes
- Pain with nausea, vomiting, marked sweating, breathlessness
- Pain with haemodynamic instability
- New-onset or deteriorating angina with frequent episodes >15 minutes

## Important Notes
- Do NOT use GTN response to diagnose ACS
- Assess symptoms identically across genders and ethnic groups
- Not all ACS presents with central chest pain

## Referral Pathways
- **Emergency**: currently experiencing chest pain, OR pain-free but within 12hrs AND abnormal/unavailable ECG
- **Urgent same-day**: pain within 12hrs, now pain-free with normal ECG, OR last pain 12-72hrs ago
- **Hospital assessment**: pain resolved with complications (e.g. pulmonary oedema), OR suspected ACS >72hrs ago

## Resting 12-Lead ECG
- Obtain ASAP; do not delay hospital transfer
- ST-elevation or new LBBB → follow local STEMI protocols
- ST-depression/T-wave changes → NSTEMI/unstable angina pathway
- Normal ECG does NOT exclude ACS

## Immediate ACS Management
- Sublingual/buccal GTN or IV opioids for pain relief
- 300mg aspirin loading dose ASAP (unless allergic)
- Oxygen only if SpO2 <94% (target 94-98%), or COPD (target 88-92%)
- Monitor: pain changes, vital signs, rhythm, SpO2, repeated ECGs

## Hospital Assessment for Suspected ACS
- Resting ECG and high-sensitivity troponin I or T on arrival
- Physical exam: haemodynamic status, complications, non-coronary causes (aortic dissection)

## Troponin Interpretation
- Use high-sensitivity troponin ONLY when ACS is suspected
- Account for: clinical presentation, symptom onset timing, ECG findings, pre-test probability
- Positive first test does not necessarily confirm MI

## Universal Definition of MI Requires
- Troponin rise/fall with ≥1 value above 99th percentile AND at least one of:
  - Ischaemia symptoms
  - New/presumed new ST-T changes or LBBB
  - Pathological Q waves
  - New imaging evidence of myocardial loss or wall motion abnormality

## Before Confirming ACS with Elevated Troponin
- Exclude: myocarditis, aortic dissection, pulmonary embolism

## Stable Angina Classification
- **Typical angina**: 3 features — constricting discomfort, exertion-triggered, relieved by rest/GTN within ~5 min
- **Atypical angina**: 2 of above features
- **Non-anginal chest pain**: 0-1 features
- Features suggesting NOT angina: continuous/prolonged pain, unrelated to activity, pleuritic

## Stable Angina Diagnostic Pathway
- First-line: 64-slice CT coronary angiography
- Second-line: non-invasive functional imaging (SPECT, stress echo, MR perfusion)
- Third-line: invasive coronary angiography
- Do NOT use exercise ECG for diagnosis without known CAD
