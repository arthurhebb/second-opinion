# COPD Exacerbation — NICE NG115

## Definition
- Sustained worsening of symptoms beyond normal day-to-day variations
- Acute onset requiring a change in regular medication

## Symptoms
- Worsening breathlessness
- Increased cough
- Increased sputum volume
- Change in sputum colour (purulent)

## Admission Criteria
- Severe breathlessness
- Cyanosis
- SpO2 <90%
- pH <7.35 on ABG
- Acute confusion
- Significant comorbidities (e.g. heart failure, renal impairment)

## Home Management Criteria
- Mild breathlessness only
- SpO2 ≥90%
- Normal pH on ABG
- No confusion
- Adequate social support

## Oxygen Therapy
- Maintain individualised oxygen saturation range
- Avoid excessive oxygen in patients at risk of hypercapnia
- Target SpO2 88-92% in most COPD patients

## Pharmacological Management
- Oral prednisolone 30mg daily for 5 days
- Antibiotics if purulent sputum (guided by local antimicrobial guidelines)
- Increase frequency of bronchodilators

## Non-Invasive Ventilation (NIV)
- First-line treatment for persistent hypercapnic ventilatory failure (pH <7.35)
- Despite optimal medical therapy on the ward

## Investigations
- Chest X-ray
- ECG
- Arterial blood gas (ABG)
- FBC
- U&Es
- Sputum culture
- Blood cultures if febrile

## Key Pitfalls
- Over-oxygenation causing CO2 retention in hypercapnic patients
- Underestimating severity — always check ABG
- Delayed NIV initiation when pH <7.35
- Not investigating for concurrent pneumonia or PE
