# Head Injury — NICE NG232

## GCS Classification
- Mild: GCS 13-15
- Moderate: GCS 9-12
- Severe: GCS ≤8

## CT Head Within 1 Hour
- GCS ≤12 at initial assessment
- GCS <15 at 2 hours post-injury
- Signs of skull fracture (clear fluid from ears/nose, black eye without direct trauma, bleeding behind ears)
- Post-traumatic seizure
- Focal neurological deficit
- >1 episode of vomiting

## CT Head Within 8 Hours
- Age ≥65 years
- Bleeding or clotting disorders (including anticoagulant use)
- Dangerous mechanism of injury (e.g. pedestrian hit by vehicle, fall >1 metre)
- >30 minutes retrograde amnesia (with loss of consciousness or amnesia)

## Anticoagulant Patients
- Consider CT within 8 hours even with normal GCS
- Lower threshold for imaging

## Skull Fracture Signs
- Clear fluid from ears or nose (CSF leak)
- Black eye (periorbital bruising) without direct trauma
- Bleeding behind ears (Battle's sign)

## Observation Protocol
- Half-hourly GCS until GCS = 15
- Then: every 30 minutes for 2 hours
- Then: every 1 hour for 4 hours
- Then: every 2 hours thereafter

## Tranexamic Acid
- 2g IV if GCS ≤12 and within 2 hours of injury

## Admission Criteria
- Imaging abnormalities
- GCS not returned to 15
- Persistent symptoms requiring observation
- Anticoagulant use with ongoing concerns

## Key Pitfalls
- Missing significant injury in anticoagulated patients
- Attributing confusion to intoxication rather than head injury
- Not repeating GCS assessment at 2 hours
- Delayed CT in patients meeting criteria
