# Suspected Cancer Recognition — NICE NG12

## Lung Cancer
- 2-week wait chest X-ray if age 40+ with 2 or more unexplained symptoms: persistent cough, fatigue, shortness of breath, chest pain, weight loss, appetite loss
- 1 symptom sufficient if smoker or ex-smoker
- Urgent referral for unexplained haemoptysis

## Colorectal Cancer
- FIT (faecal immunochemical test) for: change in bowel habit, iron deficiency anaemia, abdominal mass, unexplained weight loss
- Refer via 2-week wait if FIT ≥10 mcg Hb/g faeces
- Urgent referral for rectal mass or unexplained anal mass

## Breast Cancer
- 2-week wait referral if age 30+ with unexplained breast lump
- Consider referral for: skin changes, nipple retraction, nipple discharge

## Prostate Cancer
- PSA testing and digital rectal examination (DRE) for LUTS, erectile dysfunction, or haematuria
- Refer if DRE suggests malignancy (regardless of PSA level)

## Ovarian Cancer
- CA125 if persistent (≥12 times/month): abdominal distension/bloating, early satiety, pelvic/abdominal pain, urinary frequency
- Ultrasound if CA125 ≥35 IU/mL
- 2-week wait referral if USS suggestive

## Brain/CNS Cancer
- Urgent MRI within 2 weeks for progressive sub-acute CNS deficit

## Myeloma
- Age 60+ with persistent bone pain (especially back pain): request FBC, calcium, ESR/plasma viscosity, serum protein electrophoresis, urine for Bence Jones protein, free light chains

## Pancreatic Cancer
- Age 40+ with jaundice: 2-week wait referral
- Consider if new-onset diabetes with weight loss

## Unexplained Weight Loss
- Assess for additional symptoms and refer urgently as appropriate

## Key Pitfalls
- Attributing symptoms to benign causes without appropriate investigation
- Not using FIT for colorectal cancer screening in symptomatic patients
- Missing myeloma in elderly patients with persistent bone pain
- Delayed referral due to low clinical suspicion
