# Upper GI Bleeding — NICE CG141

## Risk Scoring
- Blatchford score at initial evaluation (pre-endoscopy)
- Rockall score post-endoscopy
- Early discharge if pre-endoscopy Blatchford score = 0

## Endoscopy Timing
- Endoscopy within 24 hours of admission
- Immediately after resuscitation if severe or unstable haemodynamic status
- Do NOT give PPIs before endoscopy (does not improve outcomes)
- PPIs post-endoscopy if recent haemorrhage stigmata found

## Non-Variceal Bleeding Management
- Mechanical clips, thermal coagulation, or fibrin/thrombin with adrenaline injection
- Do NOT use adrenaline injection alone (only as adjunct)
- Repeat endoscopy if re-bleeding before surgery

## Variceal Bleeding Management
- Terlipressin at presentation (before endoscopy)
- Prophylactic antibiotics at presentation
- Band ligation for oesophageal varices (first-line endoscopic treatment)
- Sclerotherapy if banding not possible

## Medications
- Continue low-dose aspirin after haemostasis achieved if cardiovascular indication exists
- Discuss other antiplatelet/anticoagulant management with specialist

## Resuscitation
- Transfuse per local massive transfusion protocols
- Avoid over-transfusion — restrictive transfusion strategy preferred
- Platelet and clotting factor replacement as needed

## Key Pitfalls
- Giving PPIs before endoscopy (no benefit, may mask findings)
- Using adrenaline injection alone for non-variceal bleeding
- Underestimating severity — Blatchford score 0 is needed for safe early discharge
- Missing variceal bleeding and not giving terlipressin/antibiotics early
- Over-transfusion worsening variceal bleeding
